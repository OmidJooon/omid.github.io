⚡ The fastest way to remember

Vit K (II,VII,IX,X)
"2, 7, 9, 10" — "Two Very Nice eXams"
These 4 + Protein C/S are all Vit K-dependent. Classic warfarin targets.
Serinproteases
Factors II, VII, IX, X, XI, XII are all serine proteases.
"Every active factor is a serinprotease except the cofactors (III, V, VIII) and special ones (I, IV, XIII)."
Thrombin-sensitive
Factors activated or cleaved by thrombin: I → Fibrin, V, VIII, XIII
"Thrombin activates its own amplification loop (V, VIII) and seals the clot (I→fibrin, XIII cross-links)."
Cofactors
III (Tissue factor), V (Proaccelerin), VIII (Antihemophilic F)
"They don't cut — they accelerate. No enzymatic activity alone."
Contact factors
XI, XII (intrinsic/contact pathway)
"XI = eXtra Intrinsic, XII = eXtra eXtrinsic contact... both hit the surface."
The odd ones
IV = Ca²⁺ (just an ion, no synthesis)
XIII = Transglutaminase, Thrombin-sensitive — the only one that cross-links fibrin
III = Tissue factor — the only one NOT hepatic, it's a cofactor from tissue
